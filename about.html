000 — what is this

PLAYLIST BRIDGE ABOUT

A free tool that turns any AI-generated song list into instant links for Spotify, YouTube Music, and YouTube.

No login. No account. No friction. Paste a list. Start listening.

001 — the origin

Why it exists
THE
PROBLEM
IT SOLVES

AI models like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are genuinely great music curators. Ask them for a playlist and they'll give you something surprisingly good — specific, thoughtful, tailored.

The problem is what happens next. You have a list of song names in a chat window, and getting them into a music app means searching each one manually. For a 20-song playlist, that's 20 separate searches. It's tedious enough that most people just don't bother.

OTHER TOOLS MADE YOU JUMP THROUGH HOOPS.
THIS ONE DOESN'T.

Every other solution at the time required OAuth logins, Spotify account connections, and multi-step authorization flows just to search for a song. PlaylistBridge does none of that. Paste your list, pick a platform, get links. Done in under 5 seconds.

002 — what it does

HOW IT
WORKS

01 / PARSE
ANY FORMAT

Numbered lists, bullet points, bold markdown, plain lines — PlaylistBridge strips all formatting automatically. Paste it raw, exactly as the AI gave it to you.

02 / MATCH
METADATA LOOKUP

Every track is looked up via the iTunes Search API. Title, artist, and artwork are resolved in parallel — a 20-song list is typically done in under 5 seconds.

03 / LINK
INSTANT LINKS

Direct search links for Spotify, YouTube Music, or YouTube. One click per song. No account connection, no OAuth, no permissions. Just links that work.

04 / PLAY
INSTANTPLAY

Optional in-browser player powered by YouTube. Plays your whole list sequentially without leaving the page. Lock screen controls, queue, shuffle — the works.

05 / SHARE
COMMUNITY

Publish your playlist to the community feed. Browse what others are listening to. Every session gets a shareable link you can send to anyone.

06 / FREE
ALWAYS FREE

No paywall. No premium tier. No "sign up to unlock". PlaylistBridge is and will remain free. The value is in making music accessible, not in gatekeeping it.

PlaylistBridge was built by Smit Patil, a self-taught indie developer from India. He designed, coded, and shipped the entire product solo — frontend, backend serverless functions, database, PWA layer, and SEO — using Vanilla JavaScript, Netlify, Firebase, and a lot of late nights.

The idea came from a genuinely frustrating personal experience: getting a great playlist from an AI and having no fast way to actually listen to it. Instead of complaining about it, he built the solution in a weekend and shipped it.

Within a month, PlaylistBridge reached its first 200 users entirely through organic search — no advertising, no social media campaign, no budget. It ranks #1 on Google for its core keywords and receives referral traffic from users of ChatGPT, Gemini, and other AI tools.

This isn't a startup with a team and investors. It's one developer who saw a gap, filled it, and shipped. Every feature on PlaylistBridge exists because a real user asked for it.

005 — what we stand for

THE
PRINCIPLES

01
ZERO FRICTION

The moment you ask a user to create an account, half of them leave. PlaylistBridge requires nothing. No login, no email, no OAuth dance. Value is immediate, from the first paste.

02
NO MUSIC HOSTED

PlaylistBridge doesn't store, serve, or stream music. It generates links to official platforms. Audio comes from YouTube directly. This isn't a loophole — it's the right way to build a music tool without stepping on rights holders.

03
PRIVACY FIRST

Your song taste is yours. We don't log what you paste, don't track which songs you search, don't build a profile. Paste, get links, close the tab. Nothing lingers on our servers.

04
SHIP AND LEARN

PlaylistBridge wasn't specced for six months. It was built, launched, and improved based on how real users actually used it. Real usage teaches you things no planning can.

05
FREE. FULL STOP.

No premium tier. No paywall. No "upgrade to unlock". If this tool helps you, that's enough. It will stay free.
